Discovering Sicily’s hidden spots with Anthony

My husband and I have run a travel agency in Lausanne over the past decade. They have been happy years, seeing our business thrive. Last year we observed a growing interest in Italy, particularly Sicily, among our clients. To offer truly unique experiences, we prefer first-hand knowledge of the destinations we recommend. Thus, we embarked on a week-long holiday to Sicily, making Palermo our base at the Villa Igiea Rocco Forte Hotel, which has breathtaking views of the coast. 

We also hired a private chauffeur to guide us throughout our journey. Anthony greeted us at the airport with his warm smile and reassuring appearance. He was driving a shining minivan, inside which both my husband and I could comfortably discuss and take notes about the locations we visited.

Understanding that we were not traditional tourists but rather seeking authentic experiences for our clients, Anthony offered his wealth of local insights. He followed scenic routes and introduced us to his favourite dining spots, where the food was as rich in flavour as the stories he shared about each place, creating a friendly atmosphere for us. 

When he picked us up on the second day, eager to help in our route of discovery, he produced some papers in clear and neat handwriting that he had prepared the night before. They outlined five distinct routes for us to consider, each capturing a different part of the essence of Sicily. 

Throughout the week, Anthony’s knowledge of the island enriched our exploration. His guidance also provided invaluable advice on the best times to visit Sicily, ensuring our clients would enjoy the island’s beauty without the crowds. By the end of our stay, we had a treasure trove of information, thanks to Anthony’s meticulous care and deep love for his homeland. His expertise and warm hospitality left us confident that Sicily would become a top recommendation at our agency.

“Sharing the true spirit of Sicily with visitors is my passion. Each journey is an opportunity to showcase the island’s hidden beauty and create unforgettable memories.”

Explore Palermo's most exclusive spots, curated by Anthony:

  1. 01  

    Villa Igiea, a Rocco Forte Hotel

    A 5-star hotel offering a breathtaking view of the bay of Palermo, renowned for its luxurious rooms and historical architecture. Address: Salita Belmonte, 43, 90142 Palermo PA, Italy.

  2. 02  

    Osteria dei Vespri

    A gourmet restaurant in the heart of the historic centre, known for its innovative approach to traditional Sicilian cuisine. Address: Piazza Croce dei Vespri, 6, 90133 Palermo PA, Italy.

  3. 03  

    Via Libertà

    Palermo's most prestigious shopping street, lined with high-end boutiques, designer stores, and elegant cafes. Via della Libertà, 90143 Palermo PA, Italy.

  4. 04  

    Palazzo Butera

    One of Palermo’s recently restored Baroque palaces, now a private residence and art gallery showcasing contemporary art. Address: Via Butera, 18, 90133 Palermo PA, Italy.

  5. 05  

    Ristorante Gagini Social

    A chic restaurant blending art and gastronomy, offering creative Sicilian dishes. Address: Via Cassari, 35, 90133 Palermo PA, Italy.

  6. 06  

    Antica Focacceria San Francesco

    Famous for its traditional Sicilian street food, this historic eatery offers a taste of local culture and cuisine. Address: Via Alessandro Paternostro, 58, 90133 Palermo PA, Italy.

  7. 07  

    Villa Zito

    A beautiful villa housing a fine collection of Sicilian art from the 19th and 20th centuries, surrounded by splendid gardens. Address: Viale della Libertà, 52, 90143 Palermo PA, Italy.

  8. 08  

    Teatro Massimo

    Italy’s largest opera house and a historic symbol of Palermo, offering world-class opera and ballet performances. Address: Piazza Verdi, 90138 Palermo PA, Italy.

  9. 09  

    Donnafugata

    A luxury boutique offering premium Sicilian wines and gourmet products, perfect for tasting and gifts. Address: Via Butera, 28, 90133 Palermo PA, Italy.

  10. 10  

    Mercato del Capo

    A vibrant local market where you can explore a rich array of fresh produce, seafood, and authentic Sicilian street food. Via Cappuccinelle, 90138 Palermo, Italy.

Anthony recommended hotel: Villa Igiea, a Rocco Forte Hotel

Villa Igiea Hotel, nestled along the stunning coastline of Palermo, offers an elegant retreat combining rich history with modern comforts. The hotel, housed in a magnificently restored villa, boasts beautiful gardens and breathtaking views of the Tyrrhenian Sea. Guests can wander through its grand halls adorned with frescoes and antique furnishings, reflecting the classic charm of Sicilian architecture. Each room is thoughtfully designed to provide a serene and comfortable stay, allowing visitors to unwind and soak in the peaceful surroundings. 

The hotel features a variety of dining options, showcasing the finest Sicilian cuisine made from fresh, local ingredients. Guests can enjoy a leisurely meal on the terrace overlooking the sea or a refined dining experience in the hotel’s exquisite restaurant. With its prime location, the Villa Igiea Hotel is an ideal base for exploring Palermo’s cultural landmarks and city life. Whether you are looking to relax by the pool or explore the historic city centre, this hotel offers a perfect combination of relaxation and adventure. Address: Salita Belmonte, 43, 90142 Palermo PA, Italy.

Anthony, an Italian chauffeur with seven years of experience, offers a wealth of knowledge and a passion for service. His extensive background in hospitality, combined with a genuine love for his profession, makes him an excellent choice for travellers seeking both comfort and reliability. 

Over the years, Anthony has honed his professional demeanour at the prestigious Excelsior Palace Hotel in Palermo, where he learned the nuances of customer service. Additionally, he is skilled in first aid and evasive driving. 

A lover of all things retro, Anthony enjoys music from the '70s and '80s, including bands like Fleetwood Mac and The Eagles, as well as classical performers like Pavarotti. When not driving, he practices trekking and motorbiking, and fervently supports the Palermo soccer team.
